Research Portfolio

Functional Oxides, Thin Films, Ceramic Materials & Advanced Characterization

My research work is centered on the synthesis, processing, and characterization of functional ceramic and oxide materials. I am particularly interested in understanding how compositional modification, dopant selection, and processing conditions influence structural, optical, electrical, magnetic, and dielectric properties.

Selected Research Projects

Short academic summaries of my major research contributions.

Ba and Mo Co-Doped BiFeO₃ Ceramics

This thesis-based research investigated the influence of Ba and Mo co-doping on BiFeO₃ ceramics. The study focused on phase formation, structural evolution, and structure-property relationships using XRD and Rietveld refinement, along with electrical, magnetic, and optical property evaluation.

Focus: Functional ceramics | Multiferroic oxides | Rietveld refinement

Transition Metal Doped ZnO Thin Films

This work explored spray-deposited compositionally complex ZnO thin films doped with transition metals such as Cr, Mn, Fe, Ni, and Cu. The research aimed to tune the band gap and enhance magnetic response through controlled compositional modification.

Focus: ZnO thin films | Band gap engineering | Magnetic properties

Multi-Doped Compositionally Complex ZnO Thin Films

This study examined the structural, morphological, and optical properties of ZnO thin films containing multiple dopant elements including Ba, Sr, Mn, Fe, and Ni. Emphasis was placed on how multi-element doping affects crystallinity, surface morphology, and optical behavior.

Focus: Thin film synthesis | Optical properties | Surface morphology

Nb and Zr Co-Doped CaCu₃Ti₄O₁₂ Ceramics

This research investigated the effect of Nb and Zr co-doping on the structural, morphological, and dielectric properties of CaCu₃Ti₄O₁₂ ceramics. The work contributed to understanding dopant-assisted modification of dielectric ceramic systems.

Focus: Dielectric ceramics | CCTO | Structural characterization

Compositionally Complex BiFeO₃ Materials

This project focused on compositionally modified BiFeO₃ materials and their structural, optical, and magnetic properties. The work helped establish relationships between compositional complexity, phase behavior, and functional performance.

Focus: BiFeO₃ | Magnetic properties | Optical response

Industrial R&D and Ceramic Process Optimization

As an R&D Engineer, I apply materials characterization and process analysis to improve ceramic product performance, reduce defects, optimize raw material use, and support quality assurance in industrial ceramic manufacturing.

Focus: Ceramic processing | Defect analysis | Product development
