About My Research Direction

Materials Researcher Focused on Functional Oxides, Thin Films & Advanced Ceramics

My research interest lies at the intersection of synthesis, characterization, microstructure, and structure–property relationships.

I am Md Khalid Hasan, a Materials Science graduate with research experience in functional oxide ceramics, ZnO-based thin films, dielectric ceramics, and advanced materials characterization. My academic and research direction is focused on developing materials for electronic, magnetic, optical, dielectric, energy, and sustainable engineering applications.

Core Research Interests

Focused areas that define my academic and doctoral research direction

Functional Ceramic & Oxide Materials

I am interested in functional oxide ceramics where controlled doping, phase evolution, defect chemistry, and microstructure influence electrical, magnetic, optical, and dielectric properties.

ZnO Thin Films & Nanomaterials

My publication background includes compositionally complex ZnO thin films, where transition-metal and multi-element doping can tune band gap, morphology, optical response, and magnetic behavior.

Energy Materials & Sustainable Technologies

I am interested in materials for energy conversion, storage, and sustainable engineering applications, especially where ceramic and oxide systems can improve performance, durability, and resource efficiency.

Additive Manufacturing of Ceramics

I am motivated to explore ceramic materials for high-temperature applications, including processing–structure–property relationships in advanced manufacturing routes.

Research Background

How my thesis and publications shaped my research direction

BiFeO₃-Based Functional Ceramics

My thesis and publication background include Ba and Mo co-doped BiFeO₃ ceramics, focusing on structural, electrical, magnetic, and optical property changes.

CCTO Dielectric Ceramics

I have research exposure to Nb and Zr co-doped CaCu₃Ti₄O₁₂ ceramics, with interest in morphology, dielectric behavior, and ceramic structure–property relationships.

Compositionally Complex ZnO Films

My publication record includes doped ZnO thin films prepared by spray deposition, with focus on band gap enhancement, morphology, optical behavior, and magnetism.

Characterization-Driven Research Approach

Understanding materials through structure, microstructure, composition, and properties

Structural Analysis

XRD-based phase identification, crystallographic interpretation, and structure–property correlation are central to my research approach.

Microstructural Evaluation

SEM and microscopy-based analysis help connect processing conditions with morphology, grain formation, surface features, and material performance.

Thermal & Composition Analysis

XRF and thermal analysis support chemical composition understanding, process optimization, and ceramic processing decisions.

Application-Oriented Materials

My long-term interest is to design and study materials for electronics, energy systems, sustainable engineering, and high-temperature functional applications.

Research Motivation

The direction I want to pursue in doctoral-level materials research

Future Research Goal

From Processing to Performance

My research goal is to investigate how synthesis routes, dopant chemistry, microstructure, and phase evolution control the functional properties of advanced materials. I am especially interested in doctoral research involving functional oxides, thin films, ceramic materials, nanotechnology, energy materials, additive manufacturing, and advanced characterization.
