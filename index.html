About Me

A research-oriented materials science graduate with experience in ceramics, oxide materials, thin films, and characterization techniques. I am particularly interested in developing advanced functional materials for electronic, energy, and high-performance engineering applications.

Academic Background

Graduated in Glass and Ceramics Engineering with strong academic and research interest in structure–property relationships of advanced ceramic and oxide systems.

Current Direction

Expanding research interests toward additive manufacturing of ceramics, especially FDM-based fabrication, alongside advanced materials characterization and process optimization.

Career Goal

To pursue high-impact research and contribute to the development of innovative materials for next-generation technologies through a PhD and long-term academic research career.

Selected Research Projects

A focused selection of research work on advanced ceramics, functional oxides, and thin-film materials for next-generation electronic and energy applications.

Multiferroic Ceramics Research Project

Bismuth Ferrite (BiFeO₃) Ceramics

Investigated the synthesis and characterization of BiFeO₃ ceramics using the solid-state route, with emphasis on phase formation, microstructure evolution, and electrical/dielectric performance relevant to multifunctional oxide systems.

Core Focus

Phase purity, grain morphology, dielectric behavior, and structure–property relationships in multiferroic ceramic systems.

Techniques Used

XRD, SEM, dielectric measurements, electrical characterization

Research Value

Supports the development of functional ceramic materials for sensors, non-volatile memory, and spintronic applications.

Keywords

Multiferroics, perovskite oxide, polarization, dielectric response

Thin Films Research Project

Zinc Oxide (ZnO) Thin Films

Worked on fabrication and analysis of ZnO thin films for optoelectronic applications, focusing on optical transparency, structural quality, and surface morphology as key performance indicators.

Core Focus

Crystallinity, optical band behavior, transparency, and surface uniformity.

Techniques Used

UV-Vis spectroscopy, XRD, SEM

Applications

Transparent electronics, solar cells, sensors, optoelectronic coatings

Keywords

ZnO, thin film, bandgap, transparency, nanostructured coating

High Dielectric Ceramics Research Project

Calcium Copper Titanate (CCTO) Ceramics

Studied the dielectric and microstructural behavior of CCTO ceramics with attention to grain boundary effects, electrical response, and the origin of giant dielectric properties in complex oxide systems.

Core Focus

Dielectric constant, impedance response, microstructure-property correlation.

Techniques Used

XRD, SEM, impedance spectroscopy, electrical measurements

Applications

Capacitors, electronic ceramics, energy storage-related components

Keywords

CCTO, dielectric ceramics, grain boundary, impedance, functional oxide
