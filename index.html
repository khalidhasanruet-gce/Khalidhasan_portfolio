About

I work at the intersection of ceramic processing, functional materials, and characterization-driven optimization. My academic and industrial background combines laboratory research on oxide materials with applied R&D in ceramic manufacturing. I am especially interested in structure–property relationships, process optimization, and the development of materials for energy, electronics, and sustainable technologies.

Highlights

Research Statement

My research focuses on ceramic materials, functional oxides, and thin-film systems, with particular interest in structure–property relationships and processing-driven performance optimization. I am especially interested in additive manufacturing of ceramics, energy-related materials, and sustainable material development for future electronic and functional applications. My long-term goal is to contribute to advanced materials research by connecting material design, characterization, and scalable processing approaches.

Selected Research Projects

B.Sc. Thesis: Ba and Mo Co-doped BiFeO3 Ceramics

Investigated phase transition using XRD and Rietveld refinement, and studied electrical, magnetic, and optical properties to understand structure–property relationships.

  • Used XRD and Rietveld refinement for phase analysis
  • Studied phase transition behavior and structure–property relationships
  • Analyzed electrical, magnetic, and optical performance

Compositionally Complex ZnO Thin Films

Worked on multi-doped spray-deposited ZnO thin films with emphasis on structural, morphological, optical, and magnetic behavior.

  • Explored band-gap tuning and functional properties
  • Studied dopant effects on microstructure and optical response
  • Focused on device-relevant oxide thin-film systems

CaCu3Ti4O12 Ceramics

Studied the effect of Nb and Zr co-doping on structural, morphological, and dielectric properties of calcium copper titanate ceramics.

  • Investigated dielectric behavior and morphology
  • Examined structural changes due to co-doping
  • Published findings in a conference proceeding
