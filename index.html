Md Khalid Hasan is a materials science and ceramic engineering graduate with research experience in functional oxide ceramics, thin films, dielectric materials, and materials characterization.

Biography

Md Khalid Hasan completed his Bachelor of Science in Glass and Ceramic Engineering from Rajshahi University of Engineering & Technology (RUET), Bangladesh, with a CGPA of 3.70/4.00 and ranking within the top 5% of his class.

His academic training included crystallography, materials characterization, nanotechnology, energy conversion and storage, solid state chemistry, physical ceramics, mechanical behaviour of ceramics, metallurgy, engineering mechanics, and thermodynamics.

He has research experience in semiconductor materials, functional oxide ceramics, transition-metal-doped ZnO thin films, BiFeO3-based multiferroics, and dielectric ceramics. He is currently engaged in ceramic industrial R&D focused on process optimization and material performance improvement.

Selected Research Projects

Ba and Mo Co-doped BiFeO3 Ceramics

Undergraduate thesis research on the influence of Ba and Mo co-doping on the structural, electrical, magnetic, and optical properties of BiFeO3 ceramics. The work emphasized phase development, property modification, and structure-property interpretation in multiferroic ceramic systems.

Transition Metal Doped ZnO Thin Films

Research on spray-deposited transition-metal-doped ZnO thin films, focusing on band gap tuning, magnetic response, morphology, and the role of multi-element doping in modifying functional properties.

Nb and Zr Co-doped CCTO Ceramics

Study of the structural, morphological, and dielectric properties of Nb and Zr co-doped CaCu3Ti4O12 ceramics, with attention to phase formation, microstructure, and dielectric behavior.

Ceramic Manufacturing and Process Optimization

Industrial R&D experience involving raw material evaluation, ceramic body formulation, defect analysis, process improvement, thermal behavior, and quality enhancement in ceramic manufacturing.
