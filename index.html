Md Khalid Hasan is a materials science and ceramic engineering graduate with research experience in functional oxide ceramics, thin films, dielectric materials, and materials characterization. His academic work focuses on the relationship between chemical composition, processing conditions, phase formation, microstructure, and functional properties of advanced materials.

His research background includes compositionally complex ZnO thin films, BiFeO3-based multiferroic ceramics, CCTO-based dielectric ceramics, and ceramic processing. He is interested in pursuing graduate research in advanced functional materials, ceramic materials, electronic materials, and sustainable materials processing.

Selected Research Projects

Ba and Mo Co-doped BiFeO3 Ceramics

Undergraduate thesis research on the influence of Ba and Mo co-doping on the structural, electrical, magnetic, and optical properties of BiFeO3 ceramics. The work emphasized phase development, property modification, and the interpretation of structure-property relationships in multiferroic ceramic systems.

Transition Metal Doped ZnO Thin Films

Research on spray-deposited transition-metal-doped ZnO thin films, focusing on band gap tuning, magnetic response, morphology, and the effect of multi-element doping on functional properties.

Nb and Zr Co-doped CCTO Ceramics

Investigation of the structural, morphological, and dielectric properties of Nb and Zr co-doped CaCu3Ti4O12 ceramics, with attention to phase formation, microstructure, and dielectric behavior.

Ceramic Manufacturing and Process Optimization

Industrial R&D experience involving raw material evaluation, ceramic body formulation, defect analysis, process improvement, thermal behavior, and quality enhancement in ceramic manufacturing.
