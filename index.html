Selected Research Projects

Research presented with stronger depth and clarity

These projects reflect your strongest materials science themes from thesis, publications, and industry work.

Thesis BiFeO₃

Ba and Mo Co-doped BiFeO₃ Ceramics

Bachelor’s thesis focused on the influence of Ba and Mo co-doping on the structural, electrical, magnetic, and optical properties of BiFeO₃ ceramics.

  • Investigated phase transition using XRD and Rietveld refinement
  • Studied structure-property relationships
  • Analyzed electrical, magnetic, and optical behavior
Thin Films ZnO

Compositionally Complex ZnO Thin Films

Research on multi-doped and transition-metal-doped ZnO thin films with emphasis on structural, morphological, optical, and magnetic properties.

  • Spray-deposited thin film development
  • Band gap and magnetism enhancement studies
  • Surface and morphology-related property investigation
Dielectrics CCTO

Nb and Zr Co-doped CCTO Ceramics

Investigation of structural, morphological, and dielectric properties of CaCu₃Ti₄O₁₂ ceramics through co-doping strategies.

  • Focused on dielectric response
  • Microstructure-property understanding
  • Relevant to capacitor and electronic material systems
Industry R&D

Ceramic Process Optimization & Product Performance

Industrial R&D work centered on material efficiency, process optimization, defect analysis, and quality improvement in ceramic manufacturing.

  • XRF and dilatometer-based analysis
  • Raw material utilization improvement
  • Root cause analysis for product defect reduction
